## IV. PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO PINNACLE PLAN

Based on the scientific evidence presented, Plaintiffs request the following amendments to the Pinnacle Plan monitoring framework:

### Amendment 1: Maximum Planned Placement Changes

**Current:** No hard cap on planned placement changes.

**Proposed:** Maximum ONE planned placement change per child. Any additional planned move requires:
- Written justification demonstrating the move serves the child's safety (not administrative convenience)
- Approval by the Co-Neutrals or their designee
- Documentation of the child's current position relative to the percolation threshold

### Amendment 2: Minimum Placement Duration

**Current:** No minimum placement duration requirement.

**Proposed:** 24-month minimum placement commitment for all foster placements, absent documented safety concerns. This ensures children can cross the percolation threshold (φ_c = 0.59, typically reached at 6-18 months) and achieve self-sustaining recovery.

### Amendment 3: Keeper Consistency Metric

**Current:** Worker turnover is tracked but not tied to specific child outcomes.

**Proposed:** Each child in care shall have a designated "primary keeper" (foster parent or consistent caregiver) whose continuity is tracked as a Pinnacle Plan metric. The metric shall measure:
- Percentage of children with the same primary keeper for 12+ consecutive months
- Target: 85% of children maintain the same primary keeper for 12+ months
- Percentage of children with the same primary keeper for 24+ consecutive months
- Target: 70% of children maintain the same primary keeper for 24+ months

### Amendment 4: Group Home Phase-Out for Children Under 12

**Current:** Group homes/congregate care settings remain in use for children of all ages.

**Proposed:** No child under 12 shall be placed in a group home or congregate care setting when a foster family placement is available or can be recruited within 30 days. Group homes shall be phased out for children under 12 within 24 months, with the following exceptions:
- Acute psychiatric stabilization (maximum 30 days)
- Sibling groups of 4+ where no single family can accommodate (with active recruitment for family placement)

**Economic justification:** Group homes cost $60,000-$120,000/year per child. Therapeutic foster care costs $30,000-$50,000/year. Redirecting group home budgets to foster family recruitment and keeper training saves money while producing better outcomes.

### Amendment 5: Rescue Protocol Training

**Current:** No standardized training on attachment repair science for foster parents.

**Proposed:** All foster parents shall receive training on the three-requirement rescue protocol:
1. Identifying and stopping decoherence sources (including forced visitation that retraumatizes)
2. Maintaining consistent keeper presence (same person, predictable schedule)
3. Gentle coupling techniques (letting the child set the pace, echo-response interaction)

Training shall be developed in consultation with trauma-informed care experts and shall be completed within 90 days of licensure.
