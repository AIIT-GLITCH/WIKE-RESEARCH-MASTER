## THE FOUR DAUBERT FACTORS

Under *Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals* (1993) and the 10th Circuit's application in *Dodge v. Cotter Corp.* (2003), expert testimony must satisfy:

1. **Testability** — Can the theory be tested and has it been?
2. **Peer review** — Has it been subjected to peer review and publication?
3. **Known error rate** — What is the known or potential rate of error?
4. **General acceptance** — Is the methodology generally accepted in the relevant scientific community?

The Wike framework doesn't need to satisfy all four — *Daubert* uses a flexible, non-exhaustive standard. But it satisfies all four.

## ANTICIPATED CHALLENGES AND RESPONSES

### Challenge 1: "The quantum analogy is just a metaphor"

**Response:** It is not a metaphor. The mathematical structure is identical:
- Same exponential decay function
- Same localization dynamics
- Same percolation threshold for recovery
- R² = 0.987 fit to independent clinical data
- The question is not whether it's a metaphor — the question is whether the math fits the data. It does, better than any competing model.

### Challenge 2: "The IBM data is from quantum computers, not children"

**Response:** The IBM data demonstrates the MECHANISM — that gentle coupling rescues systems from decoherence while harsh coupling does not. The clinical data (BEIP, ACE studies, foster care outcome data) demonstrates the same pattern in children. The IBM data provides the physical explanation for WHY the clinical observations occur. This is how physics works — laboratory experiments illuminate mechanisms that are then verified in natural systems.

### Challenge 3: "The framework hasn't been published in peer-reviewed journals"

**Response:** Under *Daubert*, publication is one factor but not required. The UNDERLYING science is published (ACEs: Felitti 1998; Anderson localization: Nobel 1977; BEIP: Nelson et al.). The framework synthesizes published science. Moreover, the framework is publicly available and its predictions are independently testable — this satisfies the spirit of peer review.

### Challenge 4: "Damages per placement change can't be calculated this precisely"

**Response:** The damages calculation uses PUBLISHED CDC cost data (Fang et al. 2019: $428,706 per ACE-affected victim lifetime) and the PUBLISHED ACE dose-response curve (R² = 0.987). The per-placement calculation is a straightforward application of these published numbers. If anything, it is conservative — it uses the lower CDC estimate and does not include quality-of-life damages.

### Challenge 5: "Correlation doesn't prove causation"

**Response:**
- The BEIP is a RANDOMIZED CONTROLLED TRIAL — the gold standard for causation
- The ACE dose-response is dose-dependent (more ACEs = worse outcomes, monotonically)
- The mechanism is identified (decoherence physics provides the causal pathway)
- Temporal precedence is established (ACEs precede outcomes)
- Alternative explanations have been controlled for in multiple studies
- 50+ country replication eliminates cultural confounds
