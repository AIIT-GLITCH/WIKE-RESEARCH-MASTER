## PHASE 3: FILING (Months 2-6)

### 3.1 Priority Filing #1: Foster Care Placement Stability
**Jurisdiction:** Federal court, state with active foster care litigation
**Theory:** 42 U.S.C. § 1983 — deliberate indifference to known harm from placement instability
**Class:** All children in state foster care who experienced 3+ placements
**Evidence:** Paper 110 rescue protocol, IBM data, ACE dose-response, longitudinal placement data
**Relief sought:**
- Injunction: Maximum 1 planned placement change per child
- Policy: Minimum 24-month placement commitment before review
- Training: All foster parents trained in keeper coupling (gentle, consistent)
- Monitoring: Annual coherence outcome metrics for all foster children
- Damages: Individual damages for class members based on excess placements × $63,000-105,000

### 3.2 Priority Filing #2: Group Home Phase-Out
**Jurisdiction:** States where M.D. v. Abbott or similar rulings exist
**Theory:** Deliberate indifference — continuing to use group homes when science proves they fail
**Class:** All children placed in group homes when family-based alternatives were available
**Evidence:** Paper 19 (one keeper > many), Paper 110 (consistency requirement), M.D. v. Abbott findings
**Relief sought:**
- Injunction: No child placed in group home when foster family available
- Timeline: 5-year phase-out of group homes for all children under 12
- Investment: Redirect group home funding to foster family recruitment + keeper training

### 3.3 Priority Filing #3: Forced Visitation Injunction
**Jurisdiction:** Federal court or state family court (seeking policy change)
**Theory:** Court-ordered visitation with abuser constitutes measurable harm to child in rescue
**Evidence:** Paper 110 (Requirement 1: harsh must stop completely), IBM data (partial harsh = near-zero rescue)
**Relief sought:**
- Default: No contact with substantiated abuser during active rescue placement
- Burden shift: Abuser must prove contact benefits child (clear and convincing evidence)
- Expert testimony: Coherence measurement before and after visitation (biomarker: HRV, cortisol trajectory)
