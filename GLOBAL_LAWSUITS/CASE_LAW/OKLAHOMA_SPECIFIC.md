## 1. D.G. v. Henry / D.G. v. Drummond

**Filed:** 2008, U.S. District Court, Northern District of Oklahoma
**Class:** Children in Oklahoma DHS custody
**Allegations:** Systemic failures — caseloads, placement instability, maltreatment in care
**Settlement:** Pinnacle Plan (2012)
**Co-Neutrals:** Kevin Ryan and Eileen Crummy (court-appointed monitors)

### Pinnacle Plan Metrics Tracked
- Caseload ratios (caseworkers per child)
- Placement stability
- Maltreatment in care rates
- Shelter usage reduction
- Therapeutic foster care capacity

### Status — CASE CLOSED (March 13, 2025)
- **Judge Gregory Frizzell terminated the Consent Order** on consent of all parties and Co-Neutrals
- Children in custody dropped from 11,000+ (2014) to ~5,800 (March 2025)
- Fred Dorwart called it a "complete turnaround"
- **BUT BY 2026:** Licensed foster homes dropped from 4,897 (2019) to 3,820 (2025)
- **Children being placed in Airbnbs, short-term rentals, and government offices**
- **No federal monitors are watching anymore**
- See: STRATEGY_UPDATE_DG_CLOSED.md for new filing approach

| Metric | Value | Source |

### Key Problems Identified
1. Worker turnover — high caseworker attrition means children get new workers frequently
2. Placement instability — children moved multiple times
3. Over-reliance on shelters and group homes
4. Underfunding relative to caseload demands

## 3. Oklahoma ACE Data

- Oklahoma participated in CDC BRFSS ACE module
- **Oklahoma consistently ranks among states with HIGHEST ACE prevalence**
- Oklahoma Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services (ODMHSAS) publishes state ACE data
- State has an ACEs initiative through ODMHSAS

### Tribal Dimension
- **Oklahoma has the largest tribal population of any state**
- ICWA compliance is especially significant
- Oklahoma tribes operate their own child welfare programs
- **Brackeen v. Haaland** (Supreme Court, June 2023) upheld ICWA — major implications for Oklahoma
- Unique state-tribal compacts regarding child welfare jurisdiction

## 4. Oklahoma Legislative Activity

- Multiple pieces of legislation related to trauma-informed care and ACEs awareness
- Foster care reform bills introduced periodically (often tied to Pinnacle Plan compliance)
- **No ACE screening mandate yet** (unlike California)
