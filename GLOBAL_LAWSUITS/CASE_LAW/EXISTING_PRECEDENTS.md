## 1. FOSTER CARE SYSTEM LAWSUITS (Constitutional Claims — 42 U.S.C. § 1983)

### M.D. v. Abbott (S.D. Tex., 2011 → ruling 2015 → 5th Circuit affirmed 2018)
- **What happened:** Federal court found Texas's foster care system violated children's constitutional rights
- **Key finding:** Children in Permanent Managing Conservatorship "almost uniformly leave state care MORE DAMAGED than when they entered"
- **Group homes specifically cited** as causing particular harm
- **Fifth Circuit affirmed key findings in 2018**
- **RELEVANCE:** Court already ruled that the system causes decoherence. Paper 110 provides the physics of WHY. The rescue protocol provides the physics of HOW TO FIX IT.

### Charlie and Nadine H. v. Christie (D.N.J., ongoing since 1999)
- **What happened:** Class action challenging New Jersey's child welfare system
- **Key issue:** Over-reliance on congregate care (group homes) instead of family placements
- **Court-appointed monitors documented worse outcomes in group settings**
- **RELEVANCE:** Group homes = rotating staff = inconsistent echo pulses = rescue failure (Paper 110, Section 4.1, Failure Mode #2)

### Connor B. v. Patrick (D. Mass., 2010)
- **What happened:** Challenge to Massachusetts foster care system
- **Key issues:** Placement instability and use of congregate care
- **Settlement required reduction in group home placements**
- **RELEVANCE:** Settlement implicitly acknowledged that placement instability causes harm — Paper 110 provides the mechanism

### Braam v. State of Washington (Wash. Supreme Court, 2003)
- **What happened:** Class action on behalf of foster children
- **Key finding:** Systemic failures in health care, mental health care, and safety
- **Led to court-supervised reform**

### DeShaney v. Winnebago County (U.S. Supreme Court, 1989)
- **Key ruling:** State has no duty to protect a child NOT in custody, BUT children IN custody ARE owed a duty of care
- **Foundation for all subsequent wrongful death and § 1983 claims**

## 2. MEDICAID / EPSDT LAWSUITS (Failure to Provide Evidence-Based Care)

### Rosie D. v. Romney (D. Mass., 2006)
- **What happened:** Class action on behalf of MassHealth-eligible children with serious emotional disturbance
- **Key finding:** Massachusetts failed to provide medically necessary behavioral health services
- **Result:** Court-ordered systemic reform including home-based therapeutic services
- **RELEVANCE:** EPSDT (42 U.S.C. § 1396d(r)) requires ALL medically necessary services to children under 21. As ACE screening and trauma-informed care become standard of care, failure to provide them is legally actionable.

### Katie A. v. Bonta (C.D. Cal., 2002, settled 2011)
- **What happened:** Class action against California's child welfare and Medicaid systems
- **Key issue:** Failure to provide adequate mental health services to foster children
- **Settlement required intensive home-based services and trauma-informed care**

### T.R. v. Dreyfus (W.D. Wash., 2012)
- **What happened:** Challenge to Washington State's cuts to Medicaid services for children
- **Settlement required restoration of services**
- **RELEVANCE:** Budget is not a legal defense for denying evidence-based care to children

## 3. PHARMACEUTICAL FRAUD (Suppression of Evidence About Harm to Children)

### United States v. GlaxoSmithKline (D. Mass., 2012)
- **What happened:** GSK pleaded guilty, paid $3 BILLION — largest healthcare fraud settlement in US history at the time
- **Key allegations:**
  - Promoted Paxil (paroxetine) for children despite NO FDA approval
  - **Suppressed Study 329 data showing increased suicidality in adolescents**
  - Misrepresented efficacy data and hid safety signals
- **RELEVANCE:** GSK knowingly administered a decoherence agent (SSRI with suicidality risk) to children and hid the data. Paper 110 framework: this is harsh forcing applied to developing coherent systems with full knowledge that it causes harm.

### Study 329 Re-Analysis (Le Noury et al., BMJ, 2015)
- **What happened:** Original 2001 paper (Keller et al.) claimed Paxil was safe and effective for adolescent depression. The 2015 re-analysis using actual trial data showed it was NEITHER safe NOR effective and revealed increased suicidal ideation.
- **RELEVANCE:** Scientific fraud that caused measurable decoherence in children. The re-analysis is a model for how Wike framework data could be used to challenge existing treatment paradigms.

## 4. ACE SCREENING — EMERGING STANDARD OF CARE
