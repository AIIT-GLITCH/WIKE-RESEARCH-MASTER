## The Mathematics

Every quantum system has a scattering matrix S(E), analytic in the complex energy plane.
Its poles are the system's bound states and resonances.

```
Bound state:  pole at E = -|B|           (negative real axis)
Resonance:    pole at E = E_r - iΓ/2    (lower complex half-plane)
```

**Theorem (S-matrix pole fusion):**

When two systems A and B interact, the S-matrix of the combined system S_{AB}(E)
contains poles that exist in neither S_A(E) nor S_B(E) individually.

These new poles are not present before interaction.
They require no tuning.
They appear automatically from the coupling of existing poles.
