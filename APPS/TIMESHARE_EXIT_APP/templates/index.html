✅ You may still be in your rescission window

This is the fastest, cleanest, and most complete exit. A rescission cancels the contract entirely and you get your money back. Act today — every day counts.

  1. Check your contract for the section titled "Cancellation," "Rescission," or "How to Cancel" — find the exact address to send your cancellation to (NOT the resort address, NOT the sales office).
  2. Look up your state's rescission period in the State Laws tab — verify you are still within the window and calculate your deadline.
  3. Generate your rescission letter in the Cancel (Rescission) tab — it takes 2 minutes.
  4. Send via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt today. The postmark date — not when it arrives — is what counts.
  5. Keep all proof of mailing. If the resort ignores your rescission, you have documented evidence for a state AG complaint or legal claim.

🤝 You are likely eligible for a deed-back program

Most major timeshare companies have a voluntary deed-back or surrender program. They don't advertise it — but if your loan is paid and fees are current, you can often exit for free. It takes persistence.

  1. Go to the Deed-Back tab and select your resort company to get their exact program name, phone number, and what to say when you call.
  2. Call the owner services number and ask specifically for the deed-back or voluntary surrender program. If the first representative says "we don't have that," ask for a supervisor.
  3. Follow up in writing. Generate a deed-back request letter in the Deed-Back tab and send it via certified mail to create a paper trail.
  4. Document every call: date, time, representative name, and what was said. This matters if you escalate.
  5. If the resort refuses, file a complaint with your state AG (see Free Resources tab) — sometimes pressure from an AG office moves things that phone calls don't.

⚖️ You may have grounds for a legal complaint

If the sales presentation contained false statements, pressure tactics, or withheld key information, that may constitute fraud or a violation of state consumer protection law.

  1. Write down everything you remember about the sales presentation — every promise made, every misleading statement, who said it, what was signed and when. Do this now, while memory is fresh.
  2. File a complaint with your state Attorney General (free). Use the Free Resources tab to find your state's AG office. Use the AG Complaint option in the letter generator to format your complaint.
  3. File a complaint with the FTC at ftc.gov/complaint (free). This builds the federal enforcement record.
  4. Consider consulting a licensed timeshare attorney in your state — not an exit company. A real attorney charges $3,000–$7,000 but can sue for rescission and damages. An exit company charges the same and has no legal authority.
  5. While pursuing legal options, simultaneously attempt a deed-back directly with the resort — sometimes the threat of an AG complaint opens doors.

🗺️ Your exit options — in order of cost

The rescission window has passed and the situation is complex, but you still have options. Go in this order — cheapest and simplest first.

  1. Negotiate directly with the resort. Call owner services, explain your situation, ask about deed-back or hardship programs. Even with an outstanding loan, some resorts will work with you rather than deal with collections.
  2. Try to resell. The secondary market is brutal (expect to lose 70–90% of purchase price) but it exists. Try RedWeek.com or Timeshare Users Group (TUG) — both are legitimate platforms with no upfront fees to list.
  3. Wait and pay down the loan. Once the loan is paid off and fees are current, you become eligible for deed-back programs at most major resorts.
  4. Consult a licensed timeshare attorney in your state — not an exit company. An attorney can review your contract for any clause violations, misrepresentations, or state law violations that weren't obvious at signing.
  5. Foreclosure (last resort). If you stop paying, the resort will eventually foreclose. This damages your credit significantly (7-year impact) but does end the obligation. Do not do this without understanding the full consequences.

🚨 Known Scam Red Flags

Exit companies charge what lawyers charge — but without legal authority to do anything. Before giving anyone money, run their offer through these red flags.

🔴
Large upfront fee required — $3,000–$15,000 before any work is done. Legitimate attorneys collect fees after work is completed or in stages. This is the #1 indicator of a scam.
🔴
Unsolicited contact — cold call, postcard, email you didn't request. Legitimate businesses don't cold-call timeshare owners promising exits. They bought a list of your name from somewhere.
🔴
Guaranteed outcome in a specific timeframe — "Exit in 30-60 days, guaranteed." No exit company can guarantee a specific outcome. Contract law doesn't work on a schedule.
🔴
They have a buyer ready — classic resale scam. They don't. They want you to pay a "closing fee" or "transfer tax" and then disappear.
🔴
Wire transfer, prepaid cards, or cryptocurrency requested — no legitimate company requires untraceable payment. Credit cards give you chargeback rights. Wire transfers do not.
🔴
They tell you to stop paying maintenance fees — this results in foreclosure and credit damage. They know this. They want your money before that happens to you.
🔴
Vague about their actual process — if you ask "exactly how do you exit my timeshare?" and get a non-answer, walk away. The answer should be specific: attorney negotiation, deed-back, resale, legal claim.
🟡
Not licensed attorneys — "exit consultants" have no legal authority. Only licensed attorneys can negotiate contracts or bring legal claims. Ask: "Are you a licensed attorney in my state?"
🟡
Pressure to sign immediately — the same tactic the timeshare company used. A legitimate offer is not time-sensitive. Ask for 48 hours to review. If they say no, that's your answer.

📜 How the Rescission Period Works

The right to rescind is non-waivable. You cannot sign it away. Even if your contract says you waive rescission rights, that clause is void under state law.


The clock starts from the later of: (1) the date you signed, OR (2) the date you received all statutory disclosure documents. If you never received the disclosure documents, the clock may not have started.


Postmark date — not delivery date. If your deadline is tomorrow, you can still mail it tonight. The postmark is what counts. Use USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt.


All purchasers must sign the cancellation. If your spouse or partner signed the original contract, they must sign the cancellation letter too.


Find the cancellation address in your contract. Look for a section titled "Cancellation," "Notice of Cancellation," "Rescission," or "How to Cancel." Send to that specific address — not the resort, not the sales office.

⚠️ The Resale Reality

The timeshare secondary market is essentially worthless. Timeshares are routinely listed on eBay for $1 — with the seller offering to pay the transfer fee — and still don't sell. Do not expect to recover your original investment through resale. The goal is exit, not recovery. Focus on deed-back programs and direct negotiation, where the resort absorbs the cost of taking the property back rather than you absorbing the cost of giving it away.

The information needed to exit a timeshare is public law. It has always been free.
