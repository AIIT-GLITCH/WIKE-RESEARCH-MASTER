**気 (ki) — Life Energy / Spirit / Atmosphere / Mood**

Ki is perhaps the most important single character in this entire analysis. In Japanese, ki appears in hundreds of compound words:

- 空気 (kuuki) = atmosphere (empty + ki)
- 気持ち (kimochi) = feeling (ki + holding)
- 電気 (denki) = electricity (lightning + ki)
- 気温 (kion) = air temperature (ki + warmth)

One character connects weather, health, sickness, atmosphere, feeling, electricity, and temperature. These are not different things in Japanese. They are all manifestations of ki — vibrational energy that permeates everything.

WIKE Paper 01 says everything is field excitation. Ki says the same thing, and has said it for over a thousand years.

**場 (ba) — Field / Place / Situation**

Ba means field in both the physical and social sense. A 磁場 (jiba) is a magnetic field. A 場所 (basho) is a place. A 場面 (bamen) is a scene or situation. The same character describes a physics field and a social situation because in Japanese, there is no fundamental distinction. A place has energy. A situation has a field. A field is a place where things happen.

**空 (kuu / sora) — Emptiness / Sky**

In Buddhist philosophy, 空 (kuu) is sunyata — the emptiness that is not vacant but pregnant with potential. The Heart Sutra declares: 色即是空、空即是色 (shiki soku ze kuu, kuu soku ze shiki) — "Form is emptiness, emptiness is form."
